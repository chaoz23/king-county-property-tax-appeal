# Adjustment Methodology

The sales-comparison approach: estimate the subject's value from recent sales of similar
properties, each adjusted to account for how it differs from the subject. The adjustment grid IS the
argument — every line must be defensible because the assessor will probe it.

## The cardinal rule: adjust the comp, not the subject

Adjustments are applied to each **comparable**, moving its sale price toward what it would have sold
for if it were like the subject.

- Comp **superior** in a feature → **subtract** (it sold higher partly because of that feature).
- Comp **inferior** in a feature → **add**.

Example: comp has a water view, subject does not → subtract the view's contributory value from the
comp. Comp is smaller than subject → add for the missing area.

## Adjustment categories (in rough priority)

1. **Market / time** — sale date vs. the assessment date (Jan 1). Adjust for price movement between
   the two using a defensible local rate.
2. **Location** — sub-neighborhood, busy street, adjacency effects.
3. **Lot size** — site value difference.
4. **Living area (GLA)** — usually the largest single adjustment; use a market-supported $/sqft for
   *marginal* area, not the full average $/sqft.
5. **Age / condition / grade** — quality of construction and state of repair.
6. **Bed/bath count**, **garage/parking**, **view/waterfront**, **other** (basement finish, ADU).

## Deriving the dollar amounts

- Prefer **paired-sales analysis**: two sales differing in one feature isolate that feature's value.
- Where pairs aren't available, use market-supported rates ($/sqft marginal GLA, lot $/factor,
  condition as a % of value) and **document the basis for each** in the grid. An undocumented
  adjustment is a liability at hearing.

## Reliability discipline

- Never substitute zero for an unknown characteristic. Require core comparison fields such as GLA
  and grade; omit an optional adjustment when either side is unknown and disclose the omission.
- Track **gross adjustment** (sum of absolute adjustments) per comp. Low gross = more comparable =
  weight it more in reconciliation. Flag any comp needing large gross adjustment as weaker support.
- **Consistency** (ties to SKILL.md Stage 6 defensibility): the same feature is adjusted in the same
  direction and at the same rate across every comp. Reversing a direction or rate to manufacture a
  lower number is the single fastest way to lose credibility on the entire packet.

## Reconciliation → opinion of value

Weight the lowest-gross-adjustment comps most. State the opinion at the **well-supported low end** of
the indicated range — argue the assessor is high, but only as far as the adjusted comps actually
support. If a likely opposing (high) comp was flagged in Stage 3, the distinguishing note explains
why it overstates the subject rather than ignoring it.
